What is particle swarm optimisation?

Particle swarm optimisation is a heuristic algorithm that has evolved from artificial life techniques. Particle swarm optimisations uses simple autonomous agents to explore a search space. Each agent simply follows an equation to update its position in the environment at each time step. There are a multitude of different equations that can be used for this update depending on the problem that is being solved. A number of these are discussed in [4].

The attraction to food modelled in the fish simulator was inspired by particle swarm optimisation in that when a fish has eaten some food, this is stored as a local best for the agent, and the food creates a global best. This means that agents are drawn to either a local best or global best depending on which they are closer to.
